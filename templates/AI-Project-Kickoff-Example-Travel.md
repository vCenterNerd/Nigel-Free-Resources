## About This Example

This is a filled-in version of the AI Project Kickoff Template, using a family trip as the example. It shows what a completed one looks like once the guidance is replaced with real content.

Read it to see the idea in action, then start your own from the blank template: [AI-Project-Kickoff-Template-v1.0.md](AI-Project-Kickoff-Template-v1.0.md).

Notice what is here and what is not. There is enough context to plan a trip well, and nothing sensitive like logins, card numbers, or other people's private details. That is the whole balance.

---

## Purpose

This project helps my family plan our summer trip to San Diego without explaining the same details every time I open a new chat. It holds our dates, budget, preferences, and the decisions we have already made.

---

## What Success Looks Like

A finished plan for each day of our San Diego trip that fits the budget, works for two adults and two kids, and means I don't have to dig through old messages and tabs to find what we decided.

---

## Current Goal

Right now I am focused on locking the hotel and building a rough plan for each day. Flights are already booked.

---

## What the AI Should Know

- Traveling as a family: two adults and two kids, ages 7 and 10
- Flying out of Dallas
- We prefer walkable areas so we are not driving everywhere
- One of the kids gets carsick, so we keep drive times short
- We like a mix of one big activity and some downtime each day
- We have been to San Diego once before and already did SeaWorld, so skip it

---

## Current Priorities

1. Book a hotel near the water in a walkable area
2. Build a loose plan for each of the four days
3. Find restaurants that work for both the kids and the adults
4. Keep the whole trip under budget

---

## Decisions Already Made

- Flying from Dallas on American Airlines
- Traveling the second week of July
- Four night trip
- Staying in a hotel, not a rental
- Budget capped at $3,500 total
- Renting a car for the week

---

## Project Rules

- Treat these decisions as final unless I say otherwise.
- Don't bring up SeaWorld or a rental home again, we already ruled those out.
- Point it out if something breaks the budget.
- Build on the plan instead of starting the itinerary over each time.
- Keep suggestions realistic for two adults and two kids.

---

## What Belongs In This Project

- The daily itinerary as it comes together
- Hotel and restaurant research
- A packing list
- Reservation notes, without the sensitive details
- Activities we are still deciding on

---

## What Stays Out

- Our other trips and unrelated planning
- Work projects
- Anything with account logins or payment details
- Random ideas not tied to this trip

---

## Parking Lot

- Look into the tide pools if we have a free morning
- Compare car rental loyalty programs before the next trip
- Maybe a bigger national parks trip next summer
- Write down which restaurants were worth repeating

---

## Privacy Reminder

Treat AI like a trusted coworker, not a vault.

Good things to share:

- Goals
- Preferences
- Plans
- Project history
- Research
- Context

Avoid sharing:

- Passwords
- Banking credentials
- Recovery codes
- Government ID numbers
- Sensitive information that belongs to someone else

Use the minimum effective context needed to accomplish the task.

---

## First Prompt

> Read this project and use it as the foundation for planning our San Diego trip. Build on the decisions I already made instead of starting over. If something is unclear or breaks the budget, ask before making assumptions.

Start with one real project like this one, capture enough context to stop repeating yourself, and build from there.
